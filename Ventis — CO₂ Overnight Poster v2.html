Ventis
Smart Dorm Ventilation
Dartmouth College · May 2026
Real sensor data · SCD40 CO₂ sensor
Ventis is not an air purifier or HEPA filter. It's a smart ventilation controller that reads your room's CO₂, temperature, and humidity — and runs your window fan automatically when outdoor conditions can help.
The air in your dorm room

CO₂ overnight — Dartmouth dorm vs. off-campus apartment with central AC · windows closed · 1 occupant
⚠
CO₂ above 1,000 ppm impairs decision-making by up to 15%. Above 2,500 ppm, nearly 50%. The dorm trace below spent 4+ hours past that first threshold — every night, every student, invisible.
Satish et al., Environmental Health Perspectives, 2012 · Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory
Dartmouth dorm — Little Hall (Choates), no AC · 1 person sleeping
CO₂ rose from 494 ppm at midnight to 1,111 ppm peak, spending 4+ hours above ASHRAE 1,000 ppm threshold.
Off-campus apartment — central AC · 1 person sleeping
CO₂ rose from 629 ppm to a 863 ppm peak — never crossed the 1,000 ppm threshold.
Filtration ≠ ventilation. In a separate overnight run, an air purifier ran all night in a similar dorm — CO₂ still reached 1,177 ppm. Only fresh-air exchange moves the needle.
