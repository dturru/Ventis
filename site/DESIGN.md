# Ventis — Design & Voice Guide

The design system and writing standard for the Ventis site (`site/`). This is
the source of truth: the site reads intentional, not vibe-coded, because the
decisions below are deliberate and documented. When you change the site, change
this doc too.

Tokens referenced here are defined in `src/global.css` (`:root`). Component
classes referenced here exist in that file. Keep the two in sync.

---

## 1. Brand & personality

Ventis makes an invisible problem visible and acts on it. The personality
follows from that:

- **Honest before impressive.** We show the negative control (East Wheelock),
  state the winter limitation, and never fabricate a number. Credibility is the
  product — the dataset and the candor are the moat.
- **Calm, technical, editorial.** Closer to a well-edited field report than a
  startup splash page. Confident, specific, unhurried.
- **Warm, not cute.** Dodi (the mascot) carries the warmth so the prose doesn't
  have to strain for it.

Tagline: **Senses · Ventilates · Explains.** Three verbs, in that order — it
also maps to the product loop and the How-It-Works page.

---

## 2. Voice & tone (also the copy standard)

The site's copy is strong when it's **specific and plain**. It drifts toward an
"AI-generated" feel when it leans on a few rhetorical crutches. Rules:

**Do**
- Lead with the concrete: a number, a room, a moment ("1,111 ppm in a Little
  Hall single, four hours past the line").
- Vary sentence length and rhythm. Let short declaratives land.
- Use plain connectives (period, comma, "and", "but") most of the time.
- Trust the reader. State the point once, well.

**Avoid (the AI-tells)**
- **Em-dash overload.** At most one em-dash per paragraph, and not in every
  paragraph. Prefer a period or comma. (This is the single biggest tell.)
- **The "It's not X, it's Y" / "X vs. Y" antithesis on repeat.** It's punchy
  once per page. Three times is a tic. Vary it.
- **"honest / honestly" as a verbal crutch.** Demonstrate candor (show the
  negative control); don't keep announcing it. Aim for ≤1 use per page.
- **Triadic "no X, no Y, no Z" lists** more than once in a stretch.
- **Throat-clearing** ("It's important to note", "In today's world").
- **Generic startup-speak** ("seamless", "revolutionary", "game-changing",
  "we're on a mission").

**Mechanics**
- Curly quotes and apostrophes (`'` `'` `"` `"`), not straight.
- "CO₂" with subscript; "ppm" lowercase; "ASHRAE 1,000 ppm" as written.
- Numbers: thousands separators ("1,111 ppm"). Temperatures in °F on-screen.
- No emoji as UI or as bullets/eyebrows. Use the SVG icon set (§6).

**Claim integrity (non-negotiable)**
- Every CO₂ figure, citation, and product claim on the site must trace to real
  measured data or a cited source. Never round a measurement for punch. The
  data page numbers come from `runs.json` (generated by
  `scripts/extract_runs.py` from the real CSVs) — don't hand-edit them.
- Roadmap language: v2 = "first units expected Fall 2026" framed as a target,
  not a guarantee. No pricing anywhere.

## 3. Color

Defined in `:root`. Green is the brand; the CO₂ tiers are semantic and must keep
their meaning everywhere they appear.

**Brand greens** — `--green #1e6e3a` (primary), `--green-deep #155026`,
`--green-darker #0d4520`, `--green-soft #e8f5e9`, `--green-mist #f1f7f1`.

**Semantic air tiers** (CO₂ state — never repurpose these hues for decoration):
- Green `--green` — fresh / good (< ~800 ppm)
- Amber `--amber #b87900` (`--amber-soft #fff7e0`) — elevated / getting stuffy
- Red `--red #c6422c` (`--red-soft #fef2ef`) — high / over ASHRAE 1,000

**Neutrals** — `--bg #f4f7f3`, `--bone #faf9f6`, `--tile #ffffff`,
`--ink #16241a`, `--muted #5e6b5e`, `--faint #8a958a`, `--line` / `--line-soft`.

> The device-demo phone (`.device-screen`) re-scopes a slightly different token
> set to match the on-device app exactly (`--red #c62828`, `--border #d0dbd0`,
> etc.). That mismatch is intentional — it mirrors the ESP32 app, not the site.

**Contrast:** body and UI text must clear WCAG AA (4.5:1). `--muted` on `--tile`
passes; never put `--faint` on tinted fills for anything load-bearing.

---

## 4. Typography

Three roles, set in `:root`:

- **`--serif` DM Serif Display** — display only: hero wordmark, `.section-title`,
  `.evidence-title`, big stat numerals (`.point-k`). Never body.
- **`--sans` Outfit** — everything functional: body, lede, labels, buttons, nav.
- **`--mono` DM Mono** — data and machine voice: chart ticks, peak readouts,
  clocks, the device CO₂ number.

Scale uses `clamp()` for fluid sizing (see `.section-title`, `.lede`,
`.sticker-word`). Keep the eyebrow → title → lede rhythm at the top of each page.

---

## 5. Layout, spacing, elevation

- Container `.wrap` max-width `--maxw 1080px`; prose pages narrow to 860–900.
- Radii: `--r-lg 20px` (cards), `--r-md 14px`, `--r-sm 10px`.
- Shadows: `--shadow-sm` / `--shadow` / `--shadow-lg` — soft, green-tinted, never
  harsh black. Elevation rises on hover, not at rest.
- Page rhythm: `eyebrow` (12px, tracked, green) → `section-title` → `lede`.

---

## 6. Components & iconography

Reusable primitives in `global.css`: `.panel` (hero nav cards), `.card`,
`.callout` (+ `.amber`), `.points-grid`/`.point`, `.evidence` block,
`.btn`/`.btn-primary`/`.btn-ghost`, `.flow` (how-it-works), `.cta-card`,
`.device-*` (demo).

**Icons:** custom Lucide-style SVGs via `components/Icon.tsx`, set in tinted
`.ico-badge` squares. **No emoji as icons** — this was a deliberate pass; don't
regress to emoji eyebrows or bullets.

---

## 7. Motion

- Transitions **150–300ms**, `ease`. Used for hover lift, tier color crossfades,
  bar fills.
- Entrances: `fade-up` on `.page`. Ambient: hero Dodi `dodi-float`.
- Demo Dodi (`DodiMascot.tsx`) reacts to live state: breathing bob, emotion eyes
  (calm/alert/distress by CO₂ tier), wing-flap while the fan runs.
- **Always gate motion behind `@media (prefers-reduced-motion: reduce)`** — the
  Dodi animations already do. Any new looping animation must too.

---

## 8. Accessibility checklist (per change)

- [ ] Text contrast ≥ 4.5:1 (3:1 for ≥24px bold).
- [ ] Interactive elements are real `<button>`/`<a>`, keyboard-reachable, with a
      visible focus state.
- [ ] Images have descriptive `alt`; decorative SVGs `aria-hidden`.
- [ ] Looping/auto motion respects `prefers-reduced-motion`.
- [ ] Tap targets ≥ ~40px on the controls people actually use.

---

## 9. Pre-ship checklist

Adapted from the UI/UX pre-delivery discipline + Ventis specifics. Run before any
push to `main` (which auto-deploys to Vercel).

- [ ] `npm run build` passes (tsc + vite, no type errors).
- [ ] Render-verified in the browser at the route you changed — not just a green
      build. Check mobile width (≤640px) too.
- [ ] No emoji as icons; SVG icon set used.
- [ ] Transitions 150–300ms; reduced-motion respected.
- [ ] Contrast + focus states intact (§8).
- [ ] Responsive at 375 / 768 / 1024 / 1440.
- [ ] **Copy passes §2** — em-dash density low, no repeated "X vs Y", "honest"
      ≤1/page, no emoji eyebrows.
- [ ] **Every number and claim traces to real data / a cited source** (§2).
- [ ] No pricing; v2 framed as a Fall 2026 target.

---

## 10. Influences

Quality bar informed by the `ui-ux-pro-max-skill` pre-delivery checklist
(no-emoji-icons, 150–300ms transitions, 4.5:1 contrast, reduced-motion,
375/768/1024/1440 breakpoints) and anti-patterns (no AI purple/pink gradients;
no harsh neon). Ventis intentionally diverges toward a quiet editorial palette
rather than a trendy style — the credibility of the data is the aesthetic.
